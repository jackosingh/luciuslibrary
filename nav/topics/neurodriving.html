There is a transference of the sense of self when we get into the driver's seat of a car. There is this sublte intimacy, a grandiose trust. Our mind and body expand to novel boundries, those of the rims, front and rear bumper and our blind corners. Our vision of the road transmits itself to subtle adjustments of the (steering) wheel, which in turn, provides us sensory feedback, further refining the steering. This extension of our corporal self seems somewhat universal. The car is a framework designed and refined to fit our biology and reflexes.
